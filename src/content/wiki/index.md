## Concepts

| Page | Summary |
|------|---------|
| [Volo Ergo Sum](concepts/volo-ergo-sum.md) | "I want, therefore I am" — the claim that wanting, not thinking, is the most fundamental observable fact of being alive. |
| [The Three Blades](concepts/the-three-blades.md) | The three primordial cuts a wanting creature makes to become operative in the world: self/non-self (First Blade), world/model (Second Blade), and good/bad (Third Blade). |
| [Thingification](concepts/thingification.md) | The process by which continuous reality is made discrete and usable: naming regions of fog, assigning them edges, and then treating the names as if they were the territory. |
| [The MMM Loop](concepts/the-mmm-loop.md) | The recursive three-step growth loop — Measure, Model, Manifest — that runs at every scale from single-cell life to human civilisation. |
| [Foundational Dualities](concepts/foundational-dualities.md) | A layered taxonomy of the binary cuts finite minds make, organised from the most primitive (bodily/organismic) through cognitive and social to existential — each layer's dualities making the next layer possible. |
| [Information / Physical Duality](concepts/information-physical-duality.md) | Both the physical world (mass, energy, conserved quantities) and the informational world (beliefs, models, meanings, norms, institutions) are real, coupled, and irreducible to each other — and human life is lived at their interface. |
| [Layered Realism](concepts/layered-realism.md) | The book's operative ontology: reality is not one kind of thing but a warehouse with different loading rules — concrete, artifact, social, abstract, and subjective layers each real in their own mode. |
| [The Denial of Death (Becker)](concepts/denial-of-death.md) | Ernest Becker's argument that human culture, meaning-making, and civilisation are structured responses to the uniquely human awareness of inevitable death — the source of what UNNATURAL calls our "unnaturalness." |

## Themes
